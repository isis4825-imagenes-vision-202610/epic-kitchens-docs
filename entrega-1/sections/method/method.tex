\section{Methodology and Proposed Focus}

\subsection{Technical focus}

This project will begin with a thorough analysis of the dataset, presented in the following section. During this stage, we will identify its structure, composition and possible pre-processing filters that can be applied to enhance the training data.

Then, we will begin with the design of an ML/DL architecture based on the foundational models established in EgoVLP~\cite{lin2022egovlp}, LAVILA~\cite{zhao2023lavila} and JPoSE~\cite{wray2019jpose}. Starting off from foundational models proves useful in fine-tuning and will enable an exploration of the impact of processing in multimodal retrieval. This architecture uses a dual-encoder projected on a shared representation space and SMS-Loss over the soft-label relevance matrices as the loss function.

To implement and experiment with this architecture, we will use a modern and robust MLOps pipeline that pulls weights from AVION ViT-L/14~\cite{zhao2023avion}, SMS-Loss~\cite{wang2024symmetricmultisimilaritylossepickitchens100} and CR-CLIP~\cite{he2025crclip}, the two previous winner build on the aforementioned architecure.  Experiment management will be done with \href{https://hydra.cc/}{HYDRA} and composable YAML files. Due to the GPU-intensiveness of these models, this implementation will be deployed on the cloud and potentially integrate into Universidad de los Andes' cluster.

Overall, this pipeline will enable quick experimentation and provide an organized structure for the analysis of the metrics of interest (nDCG and mAP) in order to select and iterate on the best results.
